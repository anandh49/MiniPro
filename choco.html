Delicious Chocolate Cake

The history of chocolate cake goes back to the 17th century, when cocoa powder from the Americas was added to traditional cake recipes.

In 1828, Coenraad van Houten of the Netherlands developed a mechanical method for extracting the fat from cacao liquor, resulting in cacao butter and the partly defatted cacao, a compacted mass of solids that could be sold as "rock cacao" or ground into powder.The processes transformed chocolate from an exclusive luxury to an inexpensive daily snack.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 3/4 cups sugar
  • 3/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water

    • Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  • In a bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt.
  • Add milk, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla. Beat until well combined.
  • Stir in boiling water (batter will be thin).
  • Pour batter evenly into prepared pans and bake for 30-35 minutes.
  • Cool cakes in pans for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.
  • Frost as desired and enjoy!

    Tips & Tricks!

    Use black cocoa powder.

    What is black cocoa powder? Also known as Dutch processed cocoa powder, it's an ultra processed version of cocoa powder. This extra processing creates a lower acid, smoother flavor cocoa powder that's almost completely black. If you want a super dark, smooth chocolate cake, try this trick.
